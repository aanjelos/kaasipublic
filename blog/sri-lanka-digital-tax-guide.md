<p>
                If you subscribe to services like Netflix, Spotify, or use online tools for work, a change is on the horizon for your monthly bills. The Sri Lankan government is preparing to implement a new 18% Value Added Tax (VAT) on digital services provided by non-resident companies. In simple terms, many of the international online services you use every day are expected to get more expensive in the near future.
              </p>
              <p>
                This isn't just a small change; it's a future expense you need to prepare for. Let's break down which services are likely to be affected and what you can do to stay on top of your budget.
              </p>

              <h2>Which Services Will Be Affected?</h2>
              <p>
                The tax is expected to apply to a wide range of digital services. While companies will roll out the changes at different times, you should anticipate a price increase on most of your international subscriptions. Here’s a list of common services that will likely be impacted:
              </p>
              
              <h3>Entertainment & Streaming</h3>
              <ul class="list-disc list-inside">
                <li><strong>Video Streaming:</strong> Netflix, YouTube Premium, Amazon Prime Video</li>
                <li><strong>Music Streaming:</strong> Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music</li>
                <li><strong>Gaming:</strong> Steam game purchases, PlayStation Store purchases</li>
              </ul>

              <h3>Productivity & Work</h3>
              <ul class="list-disc list-inside">
                <li><strong>Software Suites:</strong> Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Adobe Creative Cloud</li>
                <li><strong>Cloud Storage:</strong> Google One, iCloud+, OneDrive, Dropbox</li>
                <li><strong>Design & Collaboration:</strong> Canva, Figma, Slack</li>
                <li><strong>Professional Networking:</strong> LinkedIn Premium</li>
              </ul>

              <h3>AI & Creative Tools</h3>
              <ul class="list-disc list-inside">
                <li><strong>AI Subscriptions:</strong> ChatGPT Plus, Perplexity Pro, Gemini Advanced</li>
                <li><strong>Creative & Stock Media:</strong> ElevenLabs, HeyGen, Midjourney, Freepik, Envato, Shutterstock</li>
              </ul>

              <h3>Other Online Services</h3>
              <ul class="list-disc list-inside">
                <li><strong>Digital Ads:</strong> Meta (Facebook & Instagram) Ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads.</li>
                <li><strong>Marketplaces & Ride-Hailing:</strong> Service fees from platforms like AliExpress, eBay, and Uber could also be affected.</li>
              </ul>

              <h2>4 Steps to Manage the Upcoming Costs</h2>
              <p>An 18% increase across multiple services can add up quickly. Instead of being surprised, you can take control with a few simple steps.</p>

              <blockquote>
                The first step to managing any new expense is awareness. You can't adjust for a cost you don't track.
              </blockquote>

              <ol>
                <li><strong>Audit Your Subscriptions:</strong> Do you know exactly how many services you're subscribed to? Go through your bank statements and list them all out. This is a perfect time to use a tool like Kaasi to see all your recurring payments in one place. You might be surprised by what you find.</li>
                <li><strong>Calculate the New Cost:</strong> Do the simple math for each subscription. Take the current price and multiply it by 1.18. This will be your new monthly cost. For example, a LKR 1,000 subscription will become LKR 1,180.</li>
                <li><strong>Update Your Budget:</strong> Once you have the new totals, adjust your budget. Using the existing "Subscriptions & Memberships" category in Kaasi can help you see the total impact at a glance and ensure you're allocating enough funds.</li>
                <li><strong>Re-evaluate and Decide:</strong> With the new, higher prices, is every subscription still worth it? This is a great opportunity to cut back on services you don't use often. Cancelling even one or two can completely offset the price increase on the ones you want to keep.</li>
              </ol>
              
              <p>This upcoming tax is a reminder of how important it is to keep a close eye on your expenses. By taking a proactive approach, you can absorb this change without letting it derail your financial goals. Stay aware, track your <span class="text-orange-500 font-semibold">කාසි</span>, and make sure your money is working for you.</p>
