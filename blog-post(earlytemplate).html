5 Simple Budgeting Tips for Young Professionals in Sri Lanka

Aanjelo Salgado
• June 28, 2025

Starting your career is an exhilarating milestone. You've landed your first job, and the promise of financial independence is finally within reach. But managing that first salary, especially in the context of Sri Lanka's dynamic economy, can be a challenge. Here are five simple, practical budgeting tips to get you started on the right foot.

1. Track Everything (Yes, Everything)

You can't manage what you don't measure. The very first step is to understand where your money is going. For one month, track every single expense, from your daily commute and lunch to that impulse coffee or kottu with friends. "Awareness is the greatest agent for change." - Eckhart Tolle. This applies to your finances, too. Knowing you spend LKR 5,000 a month on ride-hailing might be the wake-up call you need.

2. Embrace the 50/30/20 Rule as a Starting Point

The 50/30/20 rule is a fantastic, simple framework for beginners. It suggests allocating your after-tax income as follows:

  • 50% on Needs: This includes rent, utilities, groceries, transportation for work, and any loan payments. These are your essential survival costs.
  • 30% on Wants: This is the fun stuff! Dining out, shopping, hobbies, subscriptions like Netflix, and entertainment.
  • 20% on Savings & Debt Repayment: This is your future. Allocate this portion to an emergency fund, investments, or paying off any high-interest debt you might have.

This isn't a strict law, but a guideline. Adjust the percentages based on your income and financial goals, but always prioritize saving something.

3. Automate Your Savings

Don't trust your willpower. The easiest way to save is to make it automatic. Set up a standing order with your bank to transfer a fixed amount from your salary account to a separate savings account on the day you get paid. Pay yourself first. Even if you start with a small amount like LKR 2,000 or LKR 5,000, the habit is more important than the amount initially.

4. Create "Sinking Funds" for Big Expenses

Know you'll need to buy a new phone in a year? Planning a trip? Instead of being surprised by these large expenses, plan for them. A sinking fund is a mini-savings account for a specific goal. If a new phone costs LKR 120,000, you can save LKR 10,000 every month for a year. This prevents you from dipping into your emergency savings or going into debt for predictable purchases.

You can track these easily by creating a "Savings" category in Kaasi and adding descriptions like "Phone Fund" or "Vacation Fund" when you move money.

5. Review and Adjust Monthly

A budget is not a "set it and forget it" document. It's a living plan. At the end of each month, take 30 minutes to review your spending against your budget. Did you overspend on food? Underspend on entertainment? Life changes, and your budget should be flexible enough to change with it. This monthly check-in is crucial for staying on track and refining your financial plan over time.

By implementing these simple tips, you can build a strong financial foundation that will serve you throughout your career. Happy budgeting!
